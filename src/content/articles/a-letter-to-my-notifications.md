Dear Notifications,

We need to talk about boundaries.

You arrive at all hours — morning, midnight, the middle of a thought. You don't knock. You don't wait. You just appear, small and bright, and expect me to turn toward you.

And I do. Almost every time, I do.

I've been thinking about why. It's not that you're important — most of you aren't, and we both know it. It's that you carry the possibility of importance. The chance that this one might matter. That someone might need me, want me, have something to say.

> You've trained me to mistake interruption for connection.

That's the trick, isn't it? You wear the clothes of urgency, but underneath, you're mostly just noise. A like. A reminder. A suggestion. An update about something I didn't ask to be updated about.

But I check. I always check. Because the cost of ignoring you feels higher than the cost of attending to you — even though, if I'm honest, the opposite is true.

## What you've taken

You've taken something from me that I can't quite name. Not time, exactly — though you've taken that too. Something more like continuity. The ability to stay in one place, mentally, for more than a few minutes. The thread of a thought, followed to its end.

I used to be able to do that. I think.

Now my attention moves in short arcs, always curving back toward you. Even when I silence you, I feel your absence — which might be worse.

## What I'm asking

I'm not going to pretend I can quit you. You're woven into everything now — my work, my relationships, the way I move through the day. Removing you entirely would mean removing myself from the world as it currently is.

But I'd like to renegotiate.

Fewer of you. Quieter ones. And when you do arrive, I'd like to be the one who decides when to look — not you.

I want my thoughts back. The long, unbroken ones. The kind that only happen when nothing is pinging.

With something like love, and a lot like exhaustion,

Me
