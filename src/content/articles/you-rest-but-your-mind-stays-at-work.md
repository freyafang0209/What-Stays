The workday ends.\
The laptop closes.\
Yet, the mind does not fully disengage.

Fragments of conversation replay.\
A sentence that could have been phrased differently.\
A colleague's reaction.\
A subtle uncertainty about a decision.

Nothing urgent.\
Nothing dramatic.

Even when time is set aside to rest,\
Sunday afternoons often feel heavier.\
The body is resting,\
but the mind is already orienting itself\
toward future responsibility.

Fatigue isn't always from the outside.\
Sometimes it is the inner hum of a mind\
that keeps simulating responsibilities.

Work then becomes a quiet cognitive residue,\
carried into personal time.

Rest begins not when thoughts are forced to stop,\
but when fewer things ask to be processed.

No new feeds.\
No background streams.\
No subtle prompts that reactivate evaluation.\
In those moments, the mind realizes\
it is no longer required to stay on duty.

As stimulation quiets,\
the mental residue settles.

Not forced.\
Simply permitted.

---

*Quiet Reflections*

Why do the calmest hours sometimes feel the heaviest?\
Because silence does not remove what the mind was carrying.\
It simply makes the internal noise more noticeable\
once the external distractions fade.
